**Effect of ntrials:**
With a small number of trials (e.g., `ntrials = 100`), the simulated probability fluctuates significantly between simulations due to randomness. However, as `ntrials` increases, the variance of the simulated probability decreases, and the estimate becomes closer to the true probability.  

This demonstrates the **law of large numbers**: as `ntrials → ∞`, the estimated probability converges to the true probability.
